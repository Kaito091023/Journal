DESCRIPTION


Our advocacy, “Empowering Society Through Responsible Use of ICT and Social Media,” promotes the mindful and ethical use of technology in our daily lives. In today’s digital age, social media and ICT have become powerful tools for communication, education, and self-expression. However, they can also be misused—spreading misinformation, encouraging cyberbullying, or invading people’s privacy. That’s why this advocacy is important: it reminds everyone, especially the youth, that the way we use technology shapes not only our online community but also our real-world relationships and society.


Through this campaign, we aim to educate individuals on how to become responsible netizens who use technology wisely and respectfully. Being a responsible netizen means thinking before we click, verifying information before sharing, and treating others online with kindness and respect. We also emphasize the importance of protecting personal data, using strong passwords, and avoiding the spread of fake news or harmful content.


To execute our advocacy, we plan to take practical steps that make a real impact. First, we will create and share informative posters, videos, and social media posts that promote digital literacy and positive online habits. Second, we will organize awareness campaigns in schools or communities to discuss topics like cyberbullying, privacy, and responsible online communication. Third, we aim to collaborate with organizations and local groups, such as the Foundation for Media and Alternatives, to help us spread accurate information and provide digital safety tips. Finally, we will encourage others to take the “Responsible Netizen Pledge”—a personal promise to use ICT for learning, connection, and social good.


By doing these, we hope to build a generation of digitally aware and responsible citizens who understand that every click, post, and comment can make a difference. Together, we can turn technology into a force for unity, respect, and positive change in our society.
